Greek Chicken Souvlaki

Punched-up olive oil marinade  ·  Serves 4  ·  Marinate overnight for best results

Prep15 min
Marinate8–12 hrs
Grill8–10 min
Pull temp160°F
Marinade
1¼ lbs boneless skinless chicken breast, cut into 1-inch cubes
3–4 tbsp olive oil
1½ tbsp fresh lemon juice
1 tsp lemon zest
1 tbsp red wine vinegar
1½ tsp dried Greek oregano
3–4 garlic cloves, minced
1 tsp Dijon mustard
½ tsp smoked paprika
1 tsp kosher salt
½ tsp black pepper
Skewers
1 red bell pepper, cut into 1-inch pieces
1 red onion, cut into 1-inch pieces
To Serve
Warm pita bread
Lemon wedges
Chopped fresh parsley
Crumbled feta (optional)
Instructions
1
Whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, lemon zest, red wine vinegar, oregano, garlic, Dijon, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.
2
Add chicken, toss to coat thoroughly. Cover and refrigerate 8–12 hours. Overnight is ideal.
3
Remove chicken from fridge 20–30 minutes before grilling to take the chill off.
4
Thread chicken onto skewers. Use separate skewers for the bell pepper and onion — they cook at a different rate.
5
Preheat grill to high heat. You want char, not steam.
6
Grill chicken skewers 4–5 minutes per side. Pull at 160°F internal — carryover takes it to 165°F.
7
Grill vegetable skewers alongside, turning as needed until softened and lightly charred.
8
Rest briefly, then serve with warm pita, lemon wedges, parsley, and feta.
Notes
Cut chicken as uniform as possible — uneven pieces mean uneven cooking.
Wipe excess marinade before skewering to avoid burning on the grill.
Don't overcook the breast. 160°F pull temp is the move.
Lemon zest can turn slightly bitter over a very long marination. For 12+ hrs, add it in the last 2 hours if preferred.
Smoked paprika adds subtle color and depth without going off-genre.
